Intro

What is BIDS Manager?

A desktop app that turns raw MRI, EEG, and MEG recordings into BIDS-compliant datasets. It scans your raw data, lets you review and fix every conversion decision in a spreadsheet, runs the conversion, then opens the result for metadata editing, volume inspection, and validation. All in one place, without writing scripts or editing JSON files by hand.

Why it exists

BIDS curation has historically meant gluing together a HeuDiConv heuristic, a dcm2bids config, a hand-written validation report, and a separate metadata fix-up step. Each tool reads BIDS slightly differently; the official schema is consulted at the end (if at all). v1.0 of BIDS Manager replaced that pipeline with a single schema-driven engine. The same BIDS schema drives classification, naming, GUI form generation, validation, and sidecar audits. No heuristic files to maintain, no engine-specific surprises.

Who it is for

  • Researchers who want a "scan, review, convert, validate" loop with no scripting.
  • Lab managers who need reproducible BIDS exports across many subjects and modalities.
  • Power users who prefer the CLI: five verbs (bidsmgr-scan, bidsmgr-rebuild, bidsmgr-convert, bidsmgr-metadata, bidsmgr-validate) backed by the same engine the GUI uses.

Why these eight stages?

Most BIDS converters collapse the middle three (Edit, Convert, Fix-ups) into one black-box run. Separating them is how we give you both a spreadsheet to review beforehand and a schema-driven editor to fix after.

Inspect and Validate are also separate. Inspect is interactive (the Editor pane); Validate is the audit log that can also run headlessly via bidsmgr-validate.
